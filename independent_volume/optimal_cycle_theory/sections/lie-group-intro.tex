\section*{Part II: Lie group control}

The preceding sections developed the optimal cycle on locally compact abelian groups, culminating in the Peter--Weyl spectral decomposition for compact groups (\cref{sec:spectral}). Part~II applies this machinery to optimal control on Lie groups, model-predictive path-integral control (MPPI), and articulated rigid-body dynamics. The exponential map $\exp : \mathfrak{g} \to G$ serves as the computational bridge throughout: it converts the additive Lie algebra to the multiplicative group, linearizes the Hamilton--Jacobi--Bellman equation via the Hopf--Cole substitution, and enables forward-only sampling at the CLT rate $O(N^{-1/2})$ independently of dimension.

Part~I provides three analytical tools used explicitly in Part~II:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item \emph{Szeg\H{o}--Verblunsky splitting} (\cref{prop:verblunsky}): decomposes the holonomy $H(f)$ into constrained and free parts. This is the mechanism behind the constrained optimal cycle (\cref{prop:holonomy-monotone}), which governs learning convergence: each observed autocovariance converts one Verblunsky coefficient from free to constrained.
\item \emph{Holonomy $H(f) > 0$ as non-Gaussianity certificate}: the Jensen gap in \S\ref{sec:chu} (eq.~\eqref{eq:jensen-gap}) references this criterion---a non-trivial periodic gait necessarily has $H(f) > 0$, certifying that the contact schedule is non-Gaussian.
\item \emph{Student $t_\nu$ parameterization} (\cref{prop:student-convergence}): the re-cycle trajectory from broad exploration (small $\nu$, heavy tails) to Gaussian convergence ($\nu \to \infty$) provides the MPPI temperature schedule---the noise level $\varepsilon$ in the path-integral sampler corresponds to the effective $\nu$ of the sampling distribution.
\end{enumerate}
