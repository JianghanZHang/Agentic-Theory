Heavy-Tailed Policies, Per-Step Viability Margins, and No Tricks}

\begin{abstract}
We revisit policy-gradient training for continuous-control locomotion and ask
which components of modern actor--critic pipelines are actually necessary.
We propose a minimalist baseline that combines (i)~a truncated Student-$t$
policy whose degrees of freedom track the active contact-mode count,
(ii)~a soft-minimum over per-step viability margins as the \emph{sole}
training signal, and (iii)~a Frobenius-norm capacity clamp on every layer.
The procedure uses pure score-function gradients through the policy ---
no value function, no replay buffer, no PPO clipping, no entropy bonus,
no DAgger. We show that the heavy-tailed policy acts as a mollifier: full
support and $C^\infty$ density smooth the contact-discontinuous viability
margin into a differentiable expectation. We evaluate on Walker2d-v4
[results placeholder]. The contribution is the combination, not the
primitives: each piece is known, but the integrated minimalist algorithm
has not been benchmarked against PPO/SAC on standard locomotion tasks.
\end{abstract}
