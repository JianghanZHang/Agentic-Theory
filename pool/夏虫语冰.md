Title. Covariant Radiative Transport in Curved Spacetime: Causal Observability, Learnable Kernel Structure, and Strong Consistency via σ-Algebras and the SLLNAbstractWe formalize a four-dimensional, generally covariant radiative-transport inverse problem under causal access constraints. The physical forward model is a linear covariant Boltzmann (radiative transfer) equation on the mass-shell phase space of a globally hyperbolic Lorentzian spacetime. Observations are defined as detector-weighted linear functionals of the phase-space distribution along an observer worldline, inducing a natural filtration of σ-algebras. We prove: (i) a precise causal observability theorem (“cognitive boundary”) stating that sources supported outside the causal past of the detector support are information-theoretically invisible to the observer; (ii) a finite-dimensional kernel (transfer-matrix) reduction under discretization; (iii) strong consistency of the learned transfer matrix under i.i.d. experiments using the strong law of large numbers (SLLN); and (iv) strong consistency of parameter estimation under a standard M-estimation framework with uniform laws of large numbers (ULLN), including a sharp equivalence-class identifiability statement. The results provide a theorem-proof foundation for “learning across causal horizons” in the only meaningful sense: learning the identifiable quotient induced by the observation operator and the spacetime causal structure.1. Geometric and measure-theoretic setup1.1 Spacetime and causal structureLet (M, g) be a connected, time-oriented, smooth 4-dimensional Lorentzian manifold of signature (−,+,+,+). Assume (M, g) is globally hyperbolic. Hence there exists a smooth Cauchy time function t : M → R, and each level set Σ_s := {x ∈ M : t(x)=s} is a Cauchy hypersurface.For any set A ⊂ M, let J^−(A) denote its causal past. We use the standard causal notation: x′ ∈ J^−(x) if x′ can influence x by a future-directed causal curve.1.2 Future mass shell phase spaceDefine the future mass shell phase space for massless particles (photons) asP^+ := {(x,p) ∈ TM : g_x(p,p)=0, p is future-directed, p≠0}.This is a smooth 7-dimensional manifold.Let B(P^+) be its Borel σ-algebra. Fix a Lorentz-invariant measure μ on P^+. Concretely one may take μ(dz)=√(−g(x)) d^4x · (d^3p/p^0) in local coordinates, restricted to the null shell; any equivalent invariant choice suffices for our measure-theoretic statements.We work in a Banach/Hilbert function space over (P^+, B(P^+), μ), typically L^2(P^+, μ) or L^1∩L^∞.2. Forward model: covariant transport with linear collisions2.1 Liouville generator and geodesic flowLet Γ^μ_{νλ} be the Christoffel symbols of g. Define the Liouville (geodesic spray) vector field on P^+ byL := p^μ ∂/∂x^μ − Γ^μ_{νλ}(x) p^ν p^λ ∂/∂p^μ.Integral curves of L are the lifted null geodesics in phase space.Denote by Φ_s : P^+ → P^+ the flow generated by L (the phase-space geodesic flow). Under standard regularity assumptions, Φ_s preserves μ (Liouville invariance), at least locally; we only require that the characteristic representation below is well-defined.2.2 Collision operatorsLet θ ∈ Θ ⊂ R^d be a parameter vector (medium properties, scattering, possibly metric parameters if one treats g=g(θ); see identifiability below).Assume:(A1) Absorption/attenuation rate Σ_θ : P^+ → [0,∞) is measurable and essentially bounded.(A2) Scattering kernel k_θ : P^+×P^+ → [0,∞) is measurable and defines a bounded linear integral operator K_θ on L^2(P^+, μ) by(K_θ f)(z) := ∫_{P^+} k_θ(z,z′) f(z′) μ(dz′),with operator norm ||K_θ|| ≤ C uniformly in θ (sufficient, not necessary).(A3) Source term η_θ ∈ L^2(P^+, μ) is measurable (or random; see Section 5).Define the linear transport operatorA_θ := L + Σ_θ − K_θ.2.3 Retarded (causal) solution operatorWe consider the retarded problem: find f such thatA_θ f = η_θtogether with a causal/retarded condition (no incoming influence from the causal future).Under standard well-posedness conditions for linear transport in globally hyperbolic spacetimes (e.g., along characteristics with bounded attenuation and bounded gain), there exists a unique retarded solution operatorG^ret_θ : η ↦ f,linear and bounded on the chosen function space, satisfying the causal support property:(Causality) For each z=(x,p) ∈ P^+, the value f(z) depends only on η restricted to π_x^{-1}(J^−(x)), where π_x : P^+ → M is the projection.Equivalently, the distributional retarded Green kernel (if represented) vanishes outside the causal past:G^ret_θ(z;z′)=0 whenever x′ ∉ J^−(x).We take the existence of G^ret_θ as a modeling axiom for the remainder; the subsequent learnability and causality results use only linearity and causal support.3. Observation model and σ-algebra filtration3.1 Observer and detector familyLet γ : R_+ → M be a future-directed timelike worldline parameterized by proper time τ.A detector at time τ is modeled by a measurable weight function (detector kernel)W_τ : P^+ → R,assumed in L^2(P^+, μ) and compactly supported in P^+ (or rapidly decaying), representing the instrument response over phase space.3.2 Measurement functionalGiven f ∈ L^2(P^+, μ), define the measurement as the linear functionaly(τ) := ⟨W_τ, f⟩ := ∫_{P^+} W_τ(z) f(z) μ(dz).Define the observation operator O by (Of)(τ)=y(τ). When measurements are sampled at discrete times τ_1,…,τ_N, O becomes a finite family of linear functionals.3.3 Filtration (information growth)If y(τ) is a random process (due to random sources and/or noise), define the natural filtrationF_τ := σ( y(s) : 0 ≤ s ≤ τ ),the σ-algebra generated by all measurements up to time τ.This filtration formalizes “what the observer can know by time τ.”4. Causal observability: the “cognitive boundary” theoremWe now state the precise version of “what is causally learnable.”4.1 Causal domain of a detectorFor each τ, define the spacetime support of the detector asS_τ := π_x( supp(W_τ) ) ⊂ M,and define its causal past domainD_τ := J^−(S_τ) ⊂ M.Lift it to phase space:P(D_τ) := π_x^{-1}(D_τ) ⊂ P^+.Intuitively, only sources on P(D_τ) can influence y(τ).4.2 Theorem 1 (Causal invisibility / exact non-observability)Theorem 1. Fix θ. Let η_1, η_2 be two (deterministic) source terms in the domain of G^ret_θ, and let f_i := G^ret_θ η_i, with corresponding measurements y_i(τ)=⟨W_τ, f_i⟩.If supp(η_1 − η_2) ∩ P(D_τ) = ∅, then y_1(τ)=y_2(τ).In particular, any perturbation δη supported in P(D_τ)^c is completely invisible at time τ: it lies in the null space of the causal observation map η ↦ y(τ).Proof. Let δη := η_1−η_2 and δf := f_1−f_2. By linearity, δf = G^ret_θ δη. By the causal support property of the retarded solution operator, δf(z) depends only on δη restricted to π_x^{-1}(J^−(π_x(z))). For z ∈ supp(W_τ), we have π_x(z) ∈ S_τ, hence J^−(π_x(z)) ⊂ J^−(S_τ)=D_τ, so δf restricted to supp(W_τ) depends only on δη restricted to P(D_τ). If supp(δη) ∩ P(D_τ)=∅, then δf vanishes on supp(W_τ), hencey_1(τ)−y_2(τ)=⟨W_τ, δf⟩=0.QED.4.3 Corollary 1 (Global causal boundary and indistinguishability)Corollary 1. Let D := ⋃_{τ∈[0,T]} D_τ be the total causal domain covered by the detector family up to time T. Then the entire measurement history {y(τ): 0≤τ≤T} depends only on η restricted to P(D). Any source perturbation supported in P(D)^c is indistinguishable given observations up to time T.Proof. Apply Theorem 1 for every τ and take the union of domains. QED.Interpretation. This is the formal content of “the observer cannot speak of ice”: the complement of P(D) is an exact information-theoretic blind region induced by spacetime causality and detector support, regardless of computational power or data volume.5. Finite-dimensional kernel reduction via discretizationWe now pass from continuous operators to a learnable finite-dimensional kernel.5.1 Discrete experiment designAssume measurements are collected at discrete indices (time, frequency, direction), represented by M measurement channels. Let the unknown “incident/source” degrees of freedom be represented by K input channels. This corresponds to standard discretization of boundary/initial conditions and phase space bins.Let X ∈ R^K represent the discretized source (input) and Y ∈ R^M represent the discretized observation (output). For fixed θ, the linearity of the forward model implies a linear input-output relation:Y = T(θ) X + E,where T(θ) ∈ R^{M×K} is the discretized transfer operator (finite-dimensional kernel), and E is a residual (modeling error, discretization error, detector noise, internal emission not included in X, etc.).The structure constraints inherited from physics typically include: nonnegativity, causal sparsity (no influence outside causal ordering), approximate reciprocity in reversible regimes, conservation constraints, and low effective rank due to limited geometric degrees of freedom.We treat T(θ) as either (i) a direct object to be learned, or (ii) a structured object parameterized by θ.6. Learning the transfer matrix T: σ-algebras and strong consistency via SLLNThis section gives a complete theorem-proof statistical guarantee using σ-algebras and the strong law.6.1 Probabilistic modelLet (Ω, F, P) be a probability space. Consider i.i.d. experiments indexed by i=1,2,….For each i, we observe a pair (X_i, Y_i) withX_i : Ω → R^K,  Y_i : Ω → R^M,and assume the linear modelY_i = T_* X_i + ε_i,where:(B1) {(X_i, ε_i)} are i.i.d. across i;(B2) E[||X_1||^2] < ∞ and E[||ε_1||^2] < ∞;(B3) E[ε_1 | X_1] = 0 almost surely (mean-zero noise conditional on input);(B4) The input second-moment matrix Σ_X := E[X_1 X_1^T] is positive definite (full excitation / persistent excitation).The σ-algebra generated by n samples isF_n := σ( (X_1,Y_1),…,(X_n,Y_n) ).All estimators defined below are F_n-measurable, i.e., based only on observed data.6.2 Least-squares estimatorDefine empirical moment matrices:S_XX^(n) := (1/n) Σ_{i=1}^n X_i X_i^T,S_YX^(n) := (1/n) Σ_{i=1}^n Y_i X_i^T.Whenever S_XX^(n) is invertible, defineT_hat_n := S_YX^(n) (S_XX^(n))^{-1}.This is the matrix that minimizes the empirical squared loss Σ_i ||Y_i − T X_i||^2 over T.6.3 Theorem 2 (Strong consistency of T_hat_n, SLLN)Theorem 2. Under assumptions (B1)–(B4),T_hat_n → T_* almost surely as n→∞.Proof. Step 1 (SLLN for empirical moments). By (B2), each entry of X_i X_i^T has finite expectation, and the matrix-valued strong law impliesS_XX^(n) → Σ_X almost surely.Similarly, Y_i X_i^T has finite expectation (since Y_i=T_*X_i+ε_i and second moments are finite), henceS_YX^(n) → E[Y_1 X_1^T] almost surely.Step 2 (Compute E[Y X^T]). Using the model,E[Y_1 X_1^T] = E[(T_* X_1 + ε_1) X_1^T]= T_* E[X_1 X_1^T] + E[ε_1 X_1^T].By (B3), E[ε_1 X_1^T] = E[ E[ε_1|X_1] X_1^T ] = 0.Therefore E[Y_1 X_1^T] = T_* Σ_X.Step 3 (Invertibility and continuous mapping). By (B4), Σ_X is invertible. Since S_XX^(n) → Σ_X a.s., S_XX^(n) is invertible for all sufficiently large n almost surely, and (S_XX^(n))^{-1} → Σ_X^{-1} a.s. by continuity of matrix inversion on the set of invertible matrices.Step 4 (Combine limits). For large n,T_hat_n = S_YX^(n) (S_XX^(n))^{-1}→ (T_* Σ_X) Σ_X^{-1} = T_* almost surely.QED.Interpretation. If the experiment design excites all input directions (Σ_X ≻ 0), then the transfer matrix is learnable in the strongest classical sense (a.s. convergence). This is the rigorous “learnable kernel structure” statement at the discrete level.7. Learning physical parameters θ: identifiability as a quotient and strong consistencyWhen T is parameterized by θ, consistency requires an identifiability condition. Importantly, in covariant settings identifiability is inherently “up to an equivalence class” due to gauge and observation nullspaces.7.1 Observation equivalence relationDefine the forward operator in the continuous model:F(θ) := O ∘ G^ret_θ.Two parameters are observationally equivalent if they induce the same input-output map on the experiment class:θ ~ θ′  iff  F(θ)=F(θ′) (on the admissible sources/detectors).The learnable object is the quotient space Θ_ident := Θ / ~.In the discrete model, equivalently:θ ~ θ′ iff T(θ)=T(θ′) (for the chosen discretization and experiment design).7.2 M-estimation setupAssume data followY_i = T(θ_*) X_i + ε_i,with the same assumptions as Section 6 (i.i.d., finite moments, mean-zero conditional noise).Define the empirical risk (F_n-measurable):Q_n(θ) := (1/n) Σ_{i=1}^n ||Y_i − T(θ) X_i||^2.Define the population risk:Q(θ) := E[ ||Y_1 − T(θ) X_1||^2 ].Define an estimatorθ_hat_n ∈ argmin_{θ∈Θ} { Q_n(θ) + R(θ) },where R is a deterministic regularizer (e.g., coercive) to enforce compactness if Θ is not compact.7.3 Assumptions for strong consistency(C1) Θ is compact, or Q(θ)+R(θ) is coercive with compact sublevel sets and the minimizer is attained.(C2) For almost every (X,Y), the map θ ↦ ||Y − T(θ)X||^2 is continuous.(C3) There exists an integrable envelope G(X,Y) with E[G]<∞ such that for all θ,||Y − T(θ)X||^2 ≤ G(X,Y) almost surely.(C4) Identifiability on the quotient: the set of minimizers of Q(θ)+R(θ) equals the equivalence class [θ_*] and is unique modulo ~ (i.e., there is no other observationally distinct minimizer).7.4 Theorem 3 (Strong consistency of θ_hat_n to the identifiable class)Theorem 3. Under (C1)–(C4),dist( θ_hat_n, [θ_*] ) → 0 almost surely.In particular, if ~ is trivial (true identifiability), then θ_hat_n → θ_* a.s.Proof. Step 1 (Uniform law of large numbers). Under (C1)–(C3), the family of functions { (X,Y) ↦ ||Y − T(θ)X||^2 : θ∈Θ } is Glivenko–Cantelli; hencesup_{θ∈Θ} | Q_n(θ) − Q(θ) | → 0 almost surely.Step 2 (Argmin continuity). By the uniform convergence and compactness/coercivity, standard M-estimation arguments imply that any accumulation point of θ_hat_n lies in the minimizer set of Q(θ)+R(θ). By (C4), that minimizer set is exactly [θ_]. Thus the distance from θ_hat_n to [θ_] converges to 0 almost surely. QED.Interpretation. The estimator converges to the observational equivalence class determined by the measurement operator, the causal domain, and gauge symmetries. This is the rigorous statement that “one can only learn what is identifiable,” and it precisely characterizes the boundary between learnable and unlearnable structure.8. Synthesis: causal boundary + statistical learnabilityWe summarize the formal meaning of “cognitive boundary” and “learnable kernel” in four-dimensional spacetime.Deterministic causal boundary (Theorem 1): For any fixed θ and time τ, the observation y(τ) depends only on the restriction of the source η to the lifted causal past P(D_τ). Anything outside is exactly invisible (belongs to the nullspace of the observation map).Discrete learnable kernel (Section 5): Under discretization, the forward map induces a finite-dimensional transfer matrix T(θ) that inherits causal and physical structure constraints.Strong learnability of T (Theorem 2): With i.i.d. experiments and persistent excitation, least squares learns T_* with almost sure convergence using only the SLLN.Strong learnability of θ up to equivalence (Theorem 3): Under compactness/coercivity and uniform convergence conditions, M-estimation learns the identifiable equivalence class [θ_*] almost surely.Thus “learning beyond horizons” is possible only in the following precise sense: measurements constrain the source/parameters only through their projection onto the causal domain of the detector and onto the observational quotient induced by the forward operator.9. Concluding statementWe have provided a precise theorem-proof formalization of covariant radiative transport under causal access constraints and its learnability as an inverse problem. The causal structure of spacetime induces an exact information-theoretic nullspace in the observation map (Theorem 1). Discretization yields a structured finite-dimensional kernel T(θ), which is strongly learnable under persistent excitation using SLLN (Theorem 2). Parameter learning is possible only up to observational equivalence and is strongly consistent under standard M-estimation conditions (Theorem 3). This framework rigorously characterizes the boundary between what can be known and what cannot be known by any observer confined to a finite causal domain.
